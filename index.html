What is the asset coverage ratio?
The asset coverage ratio is a measurement of the company's efficiency in repaying it's debts by liquidating or selling it's assets. high coverage ratio means that the company can cover it's debt most of the times.
How to calculate asset coverage ratio?
To calculate asset coverage ratio you should use the following formula:
asset coverage ratio = ((assets - intangible assets) - (current liabilities - short term debt)) / total debt
example: Company x has $345,000,000 worth of assets, $40,000,000 of intangible assets, $90,000,000 in liabilities, $50,000,000 of short term debt and $60,000,000 worth of long term debt, calculate the asset coverage ratio?
Using the asset coverage ratio formula the answer will be:
asset coverage ratio = ((assets - intangible assets) - (current liabilities - short term debt)) / total debt
asset coverage ratio = (($345,000,000 - $40,000,000) - ($90,000,000 - $50,000,000)) / ($60,000,000 + $50,000,000)
asset coverage ratio = 2.4
Why use asset coverage ratio?
Investors and analysts use the coverage ratio in order to determine the financial sovency of a company because it measures how well the company can pay it's debt by liquidating or selling it's assets.
